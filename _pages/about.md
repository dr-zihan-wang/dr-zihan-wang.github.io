<h3><a href="#" style="color: #4a9eda; font-weight: bold; text-decoration: none;">About Me</a></h3>

<p>Welcome! I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Science Policy Research Unit (SPRU), University of Sussex Business School.</p>

<p>My research interests lie in skills and labour markets, regional innovation and development, place-based policy, and informal institutions.</p>

<p>My work examines skills as a core regional capability shaping innovation, productivity, labour market dynamics, and structural transformation. Using large-scale quantitative data including online job postings, LinkedIn data, and questionnaires, I investigate how skills are developed, deployed, and transformed across places. With a sectoral focus on manufacturing and the creative industries, I aim to bridge theoretical analysis and policy development, particularly in informing place-based skills policy and industrial strategy.</p>

<p>I am particularly interested in how different types of skills can be developed beyond formal education and training systems, and how they can be leveraged to support regional innovation and industrial transformation, particularly in left-behind places and old industrial regions, through the emergence of new industrial specialisations.</p>

<p>I am currently working with the AHRC Creative Industries Policy &amp; Evidence Centre (PEC), focusing on the evolution of skill demand and place-based policymaking in the creative industries.</p>

<p>Before joining the University of Sussex Business School in 2025, I completed my PhD in Management at the School of Management, University of Bath (2022–2025). I also hold an MSc in Finance from Fudan University (2018–2020), a BSc in Economics (2014–2018), and a BA in Modern Languages from Wuhan University (2015–2018).</p>

<p>Prior to academia, I gained two years of industry experience as a Senior Consulting Analyst at Accenture (2020–2022).</p>
